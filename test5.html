Allow Companies to put Neural Networks on devices, instead of the cloud.

For the past five years, machine learning in the form of neural networks (otherwise known as Deep Learning) has entered into everyday applications. Applications in computer vision, natural language processing, image recognition, and more are entering the fold and expanding the capability and performance of many applications.

This is already being leveraged in the Cloud. However, many compelling technologies such as augmented/virtual reality, autonomous drones and vehicles, personal robotics, and IoT are promising but not yet feasible in the mainstream. The Cloud is unable to solve for the quality, speed, and connectivity that is necessary for these (and other unforeseen) applications to flourish.

Why Neural Networks?

Neural Networks allow machines to learn on a human level. Over time, machines can learn to recognize items in pictures like cats and dogs, or even detect when the next earthquake will occur using technology called Neural Networks.

Advantages Over Cloud

To reach its full potential Deep Learning must released from the confines of the Cloud and the dependency on highly-trained, scarce talent that only big Internet companies can afford. Edge deployment brings the following Value Propositions:

Reduced cost

By pushing functions to the client (e.g. smartphone) side, the company can substantially reduce the cost of cloud infrastructure.

Scalability

All services/features that are performed on the client side are naturally scalable since no new servers are necessary to support new users.

Privacy/Security

With data processing happening on the user side, less data is exposed to the Internet.

User experience

Local computation enables low response latency that cannot be obtained in Cloud-based designs due to network latency.

Deploying on the Edge

Very few deployments are happening on the Edge, for three reasons:

No Standardization

There are no standard frameworks that perform well and are easy to use on Edge devices.

No Infrastructure

There does not exist a simple way for most developers to build optimized code, so they must build custom solutions for each device they want to run on.

No Talent

There is hypercompetition for the small talent pool of AI and performance developers, driving up the costs to the point only the biggest tech companies can afford these highly sought after employees.
